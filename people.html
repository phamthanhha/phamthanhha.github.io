People

Cute Center is a tangible example of creative stimulus, rapid knowledge transfer and internal teamwork. Currently, there are about twenty projects, involving over 40 staff. Areas of expertise within the Center span across computer engineering, human-computer interaction, mixed and augmented reality, data mining, and several other fields including design and the social sciences. The Center is an open-concept laboratory educating more than 15 PhD, Masters graduate students and interns from all over the world. While dissertation topics and internships are typically focused around a particular project, CUTE Center gives students the latitude to shape their projects in way they find compelling.

Professor Masa Inakage

Visiting Research Professor, Interactive & Digital Media Institute, National University of Singapore

Profile
http://www.kmd.keio.ac.jp/en/experience/faculty/inakage.html
Bio

Masa Inakage is an internationally-reknowned digital artist, director, and producer, and is one of Japan's leading authorities on emerging technologies and digital entertainment content production. He sits on advisory board and executive board of numerous corporations, as well as serves as members of government committees. His artworks and animation works have been shown internationally at galleries and festivals. Prof Inakage is actively involved in variety of projects, including feature films, broadcast programs, interactively displays and live performance events. His current research projects include digital cinema, digital entertainment, media design, media art, and synesthesia. He holds a Ph.D in Media and Governance.

Professor Ellen Yi-Luen Do

Visiting Research Professor, Interactive & Digital Media Institute, National University of Singapore

Profile
http://www.cc.gatech.edu/~ellendo/
Bio

Ellen is Professor in the School of Industrial Design and the School of Interactive Computing, in the College of Architecture, and the College of Computing, at Georgia Institute of Technology. She has built creative computing tools, from understanding the human intelligence and creativity involved in the design process to improving our interaction with computers beyond the desktop into the physical world. She works on Things that Think, Spaces that Sense and Places that Play - a smart living environment in which computing technologies embedded in the built environment (e.g., objects, furniture, building, and space) support everyday happy healthy living. Currently Prof. Do is on leave from Georgia Tech to serve as the CUTE Center Co-Director (NUS side). She holds a PhD in Design Computing.

Dr. Masaaki Sato

Visiting Senior Research Fellow

Website
www.web.sfc.wide.ad.jp/~saikawa/index.html
Bio

Dr. Masaaki Sato devoted himself to research and development of Network Mobility system (Mobile IP, Network Mobility: NEMO) and Probe Vehicle Systems. Dr. Sato began the standardization effort in ISO as an international expert in 2000. Based on his contributions and knowledge, Dr. Sato led the ISO standards effort on the data format of vehicle information and the principles for personal data protection. At present, he is also an International expert of ISO/TC204/WG16 and WG17 that focus on "Nomadic Device ITS", especially he served convener of domestic WG17 committee.

His research interest includes: Internet mobility, Intelligent Transport Systems(ITS), personal data/privacy protection, composite platform of the Internet technology and automobile technology

Dr. Sun Jie

Deputy Director

Bio

Dr. Sun Jie works under Faculty of Engineering, National University of Singapore. Her research interest covers a broad spectrum of biofabrication technology, such as fabrication of scaffold for 3D cell culture, fabrication of bio-inspired composite coating via E-spray, and fabrication of biomimetic scaffold via E-jetting.

Research area: Artificial Intelligence, Monitoring System, Biofabrication

Dr. Nimesha Ranasinghe

Research Fellow

Bio

Nimesha Ranasinghe is a Research Fellow at the Keio-NUS CUTE Center, National University of Singapore. He completed his Ph.D. at the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, National University of Singapore (NUS) in 2013. He received his Bachelor of Science (Honors) degree in Information Technology from University of Moratuwa, Sri Lanka in 2007. His current research interests include Digital Multisensory Interactions (including taste and smell), Ubiquitous & Wearable Computing, Human-computer Interaction, and new media. He received several awards for his work including the NetExplo award by UNESCO 2014, YoungCT 2009 award at the Young Investigators' Forum on Creative Technology in Korea, and an honorable mention at the Nokia Ubimedia MindTrek Awards 2012. Besides the numerous academic publications, his work also featured in worldwide media including the NewScientist, Discovery News, New York Times, Times Magazine, Gizmodo, Daily Telegraph, etc.

Dr. Kelvin Cheng

Research Fellow

Bio

Dr. Kelvin Cheng is currently a Research Fellow at Keio-NUS CUTE Center, Interactive & Digital Media Institute, National University of Singapore. He received his PhD and Bachelor degrees from School of Information Technologies at The University of Sydney. He was previously a Postdoc at the Human Interaction and Collaboration Team within the Information Engineering Laboratory, CSIRO ICT Centre, Australia. His research interests include user interfaces and interaction techniques for interacting with large, small, mobile and multitouch surfaces.

Dr. Lu Weiquan

Research Fellow

Bio

Weiquan is a research fellow in the Keio-NUS CUTE Center and a course lecturer at the Communications and New Media Department in NUS. He is the inventor of Subtle Cueing, which is a method of attention management in Augmented Reality environments. Before joining NUS, Weiquan headed a company that developed mobile applications and 3D games. Weiquan has also taught courses in game development. Since August 2013, Weiquan has been appointed chief researcher in the CUTE Center, and his work specializes in the use of Mobile Sensors, Virtual and Augmented Reality to enhance learning.

About

  • Keio-NUS CUTE (Connective Ubiquitous Technology for Embodiments) center is a joint collaboration between National University of Singapore (NUS) and Keio University, Japan and partially funded by a grant from the National Research Foundation (NRF) administrated through the Media Development Authority (MDA) of Singapore. Operations of the Center commenced on 1 April 2009. The main objective of setting up the center is to collaborate on fundamental research in the general area of Interactive Digital Media targeted at addressing the future of interactive, social and communication media.
